Active Voice in English Grammar

Usage Formula Examples

What is "Active Voice" ?

Active Voice refers to a sentence structure where the subject of the sentence performs the action expressed by the verb. In Active Voice, the focus is on the doer of the action (the subject), and the object receives the action.

The Usage of the twelve tenses in Active Voice

Active Voice tenses The Usage
1. Present Simple We use "Present Simple" to describe something that generally truths, habitual actions, or fixed arrangement.
2. Present Continuous Simple We use "Present Continuous Simple" to describe something that is happening right now, arount the present moment, or for a temporary situation.
3. Past Simple We use "Past Simple" to describe actions or events that were completed in the past.
4. Past Continuous We use "Past Continuous" to describe the action that was ongoing at a specific time in the past.
5. Future Simple We use "Future Simple" to describe the action or event that will happens in the future.
6. Future Continuous Simple We use "Future Continuous Simple" to describe the action that will be ongoing at a specific time in the future.
7. Present Perfect We use "Present Perfect" to talk about the action or activity that occured at an unspecified time in the past and have relevance or impact on the present.
8. Present Continuous Perfect We use "Present Continuous Perfect" to talk about the action or situation that started in past, have continued up to the present and still is ongoing or have recently stopped.
9. Past Perfect We use "Past Perfect" to describe about the action or event that happened before another action or event in the past.
10. Past Continuous Perfect We use "Past Continuous Perfect" to describe an action that was ongoing in the past and continued up to a certain point in the past.
11. Future Perfect We use "Future Perfect" to describe an action that will be completed by a certain point in the future.
12. Future Continuous Perfect We use "Future Continuous Perfect" to describe an action that will be ongoing until a specific point in the future.

The Key-Points of the twelve tenses in Active Voice

Active Voice tenses Key Point
1. Present Simple Is used for habitual actions, generally truths, and facts.
2. Present Continuous Simple Is used for actions happening right now or temporary situations.
3. Past Simple Is used for actions that happened in the past at a specific time.
4. Past Continuous Simple Is used for actions that were happening at a specific moment in the past.
5. Future Simple Is used for actions that will happen in the future.
6. Future Continuous Simple Is used for actions that will be happening at a specific time in the future.
7. Present Perfect Is used for actions that have been completed at some point before now but have relevance to the present.
8. Present Continuous Perfect Is used for actions that started in the past and are still continuing or have recently stopped.
9. Past Perfect Is used for actions that were completed before another action in the past.
10. Past Continuous Perfect Is used for acctions that were ongoing before another action in the past.
11. Future Perfect Is used for actions that will be completed by a certian time in the future.
12. Future Continuous Perfect Is used for actions that will be ongoing up to a specific point in the future.

The Easy-Steps to help you understand well about the Active Voice

Learning how to use the "Active Voice" in English is relatively simple once you understand its basic structure. Here are some easy steps to help you grasp it :

1. Understand the Basic Structure

In "Active Voice" the subject does the action, while in the "Passive Voice" the subject receives the action. The Active Voice structure is :

  • Subject (who does the action) + Verb (action) + Object (what receives the action)

    • 2. Recognize Common Verbs

    Practice identifying verbs, as tehy are the action words in the sentences. Once you recognize the verb, it's easier to see if the subject is actively doing something (Active Voice) or if something is being done to it (Passive Voice)

    3. Convert Passive to Active Voice

    When sentences are in the Passive Voice, try converting them into the Active Voice. For example :

  • Passive Voice : The cake was eaten by John.
  • Active Voice : John ate the cake.

    • This will help reinforce the concept of active voice.

    4. Keep the Sentence Simple

    Use short, simple sentences to practice. Complicated sentences can be tricky when you are first learning, so focus on basic sentence structures like :

  • He reads a book.
  • She plays the piano.

    • 5. Check for Clarity and Directness

    The Active Voice is often more direct and engaging. When editing your sentences, ask yourself if the subject is clearly doing the action. If it's indirect, you may have written in Passive Voice.

    6. Practice with Everyday Examples

    Look at sentences from newspapers, books, or your own writing, and identify whether they are in the active voice. Rewrite any passive sentences into the active voice for extra practice.

    7. Practice Speaking in Active Voice

    When you are having conversations, challenge yourself to speak more directly by using the active voice. This will help reinforce the pattern in your mind.

    By using these methods regularly, you will become comfortable with identifying and using the active voice. Consistent practice is key!!

    The Benefits that you gain from learning the Active Voice

    Leaning and using Active Voice in English offers many benefits, both in writing and speaking. Here are some key advantages :

    1. Clarity and Directness

    Active Voice makes sentences clearer and more straightforward because the subject of the sentence is performing the action.

  • For example : "She completed the project." is clearer than "The project was completed by her."

    • The directness helps readers and listeners understand your message quickly.

    2. Improved Readability

    Active Voice sentences are typically shorter and easier to read.

  • For example : "The manager approved the plan" (Active) VS "The plan was approved by the manager" (Passive)

    • Shorter, simpler sentences make your writing more engaging and easier to follow.

    3. More Engaging and Dynamic

    Active Voice Makes your writing more interesting because it highlights the action and the doer. This creates a sense of movement and energy in your sentences.

  • For example : "The athlete won the race" sound more dynamic than "The race was won by athlete." .

    • 4. Better Communication

    Using Active Voice can help you communicate more effectively because it is direct and to the point. In the professional setteings, such as emails or reports, active voice makes your intentions clearer, reducing miscommunication.

    5. Fosters Reponsibility

    Active Voice assigns reponsibility to the subject. In situations where accountability is important (like business or law), active voice makes it clear who is reponsible for an action.

  • For example : "John submitted the report" VS "The report was submitted". The first sentence clearly shows who is responsible for submitting the report.

    • 6. Encourage Action

    When giving instructions or motivating others, active voice is more compelling and action-oriented.

  • For example : "Take the first step" is more inspiring than "The first step should be taken".

    • 7. Reduce Wordiness

    Active Voice usually requires fewer words than Passive Voice, helping to keep your writing concise and avoiding unnecessary complexity.

  • For example : "The chef cooked the meal" (4 Words) VS "The meal was cooked by the chef" (6 Words).

    • 8. Better for Persuasive Writing

    In essays, speeches, and marketing, the active voice makes argumants stronger and more persuasive by emphasizing actions and outcomes.

  • For example : We guarantee satisfaction" soune more confident and persuasive than "Satisfaction is guaranteed".

    • 9. Helps in Exam Writing

    If you're preparing for exams (like TOEFL, IELTS, or school tests), using active voice can help you write clearer, more concise sentences, which if often rewarded in grading.

    10. Enhances Professional Writing

    In feilds such as journalism, law, business, and academia, active voice is preferred for its clarity and impact. Clear, direct writing in active voice is seen as more as professional and effective.

    11. Increase Engagement in Conversations

    When speaking, using active voice keeps your sentences short, direct and engaging, making you a better communicator.

    12. Boost Confidence

    Writing or speaking in active voice can give you more confidence in your language skills because it forces you to think clearly about who is doing what.

    By focusing on active voice, you will find that your communication becomes more effective, engaging and professional, which benefits both personal and professional interactions.
